Meet the Founder Behind Bougie Bams

If you know me, you know I love bringing people together. Whether it’s hosting a dinner party, planning an event, or simply gathering friends around a table, I’ve always been happiest when there’s food, laughter, conversation, and maybe just a touch of over-the-top flair. In fact, most people who know me would tell you that “bougie” has always been one of my defining characteristics—and I happily embrace it.

I’m a proud 12th-generation Texan and the second of six children. Family and community have always been at the center of my life, and with most of my family still in Central Texas, there’s never a shortage of reasons to gather and celebrate.

I’m also known for my eyeglasses. Bright colors are kind of my thing. I own more than twenty pairs, and each one is just a little reflection of my personality—bold, fun, and unapologetically colorful. So, when I discovered mahjong, it felt like fate.

I was immediately drawn to the beautiful tables, vibrant tiles, and endless pops of color. But it wasn’t just the aesthetics that captured my heart. What I fell in love with most was the social side of the game. Mahjong has a way of turning strangers into friends and ordinary afternoons into memories. It creates community, laughter, and connection—all things I’ve always loved.

And that’s how Bougie Bams came to life.

The name is actually pretty simple. “Bougie” is what friends and family have called me for years, thanks to my love of color, style, and making life feel a little special. And “Bams”? Well, mahjong players know that’s a nod to bamboo tiles—a little wink to the game that brought all of this together.

Bougie Bams is more than a business. It’s an extension of who I am and what I love most: colorful tables, meaningful connections, gracious hospitality, and creating spaces where everyone feels welcome.

My hope is that every Bougie Bams gathering becomes more than just a game. I hope it becomes a place where friendships are formed, traditions are created, and people leave feeling a little happier than when they arrived.

Because life is simply better when there’s color, community, and a seat for everyone at the table.

I’m so glad you’re here, and I can’t wait to share this beautiful game with you.
